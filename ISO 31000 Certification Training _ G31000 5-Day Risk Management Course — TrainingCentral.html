Course Overview

Based on: ISO 31000:2018 — Risk Management: Principles & Guidelines and ISO/IEC 31010:2019 — Risk assessment techniques. Course documents and slides are provided to participants (PowerPoint format for internal use). The course outline is taken from the official G31000 program.

Day-by-Day Syllabus (Detailed)

Day 1 — Introduction & Scope

  • Purpose of the course and learning outcomes
  • ISO 31000 adoption & statistics (countries, sectors)
  • History of G31000 and ISO/TC 262 relationship
  • Philosophy of ISO 31000; Scope: Enterprise vs Traditional Risk Management
  • Benefits, users and sample quiz (exam-style questions)

Day 2 — Vocabulary (and revision)

  • Review of key ISO risk definitions (29 out of 51 terms from ISO Guide 73)
  • Word-by-word explanation of key terms (example: Risk Owner)
  • End-of-chapter quiz

Day 3 — Principles

  • Core ISO 31000 principles and how they drive decisions
  • Examples such as 'Best available information'
  • Sample questions, summaries and quizzes

Day 4 — Framework & Process

  • Framework components and governance (resources, responsibilities)
  • Process steps: Communicate & Consult, Establishing the Context
  • Risk Assessment: identification, analysis, evaluation — choosing techniques
  • Risk Treatment options and Monitoring & Review

Day 5 — Exam & Certification

  • Revision and consolidated practice exercises
  • Presentation of sample techniques (case studies)
  • Final ISO 31000 certification examination

Who should attend?

Risk officers, internal auditors, compliance professionals, consultants, operations managers, project managers, and decision-makers responsible for risk oversight and policy.
